Intrauterine Device

It is a small T-shaped device that is placed in the uterus by a healthcare provider. It sits inside the uterus, and the cervix keeps it in place. Once it is in, you cannot feel it. There are two types of IUDs: the non-hormonal–copper T IUD and the hormonal–levonorgestrel IUD.

a. The non-hormonal copper T IUD contains no hormones and is made with copper and plastic. The copper prevents pregnancy by acting as a spermicide (a substance that kills sperm), preventing sperm from reaching and fertilizing an egg.
