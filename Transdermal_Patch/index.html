Transdermal Patch

It is a thin, beige plastic square about two inches across that delivers hormones, chemicals that control how different parts of the body work, to the body through contact with the skin. It looks similar to an adhesive bandage. The patch has a sticky side that attaches to the skin and is placed on the stomach, buttocks, back, or upper outer arm. Patches that deliver chemicals through prolonged contact with the skin are also known as transdermal patches.
